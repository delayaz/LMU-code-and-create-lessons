Day 8 · Session 1 · Wednesday, July 1 · 9:00–11:30

Build Time + Design Sprint

Tomorrow you present. Today is your last full day to build — so most of this morning is yours. We'll check in briefly, build for a while, then take a real 30-minute break to stretch a different creative muscle before finishing the morning back on your project.

9:00–9:05 Standup 9:05–10:15 Build Time 10:15–10:45 Design Sprint 10:45–11:30 Build Time
9:00–9:05

Quick Standup

Go around in a circle. Each person says two things, fast:

  1. What am I building/fixing today?
  2. What's one thing I'm stuck on right now?
9:05–10:15

Build Time

Real, protected build time — no new lecture today. Before you dive in, know what you're aiming for.

What does "done" look like by tomorrow?

If you finish early

  • Add error handling — what happens if the user types something weird?
  • Add one small second feature that builds on what you already have
  • Go back through your code and clean up variable names so someone else could read it
  • Add comments explaining the trickiest part of your code
10:15–10:45

Design Sprint: Tech for Good

Thirty minutes, different brain. You'll get a real person and a real problem — no code, just ideas — with enough time to actually think it through instead of rushing to a pitch.

Your persona

"Maria, 68, just moved to a new city and doesn't know anyone."

The constraint

You can only use tools that exist right now, or things a beginner coder could realistically build.

5 minRead the persona and constraint aloud as a group. Quick gut-check: what's everyone's first instinct for what Maria needs?
8 minIn pairs or small groups: get specific about Maria's actual week. Not "loneliness" in the abstract — where does she go, who does she talk to, what's the hardest part of a normal Tuesday for her?
12 minSketch a product or feature that helps. Doesn't need to be technical — a napkin sketch of the idea is enough. Push for one specific detail: what's the very first screen or interaction Maria sees?
5 minEach group pitches in under a minute: what it is, who it's for, why it helps Maria specifically. One follow-up question from the room per pitch.
10:45–11:30

Build Time

Back to your project for the rest of the morning. If the sprint sparked an idea you actually want to try in your own app, this is the time to test it.
